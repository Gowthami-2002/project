FAQ'S

What is the coldest temperature in Kerala?

Unlike many regions in north India, the state of Kerala does not have a marked winter period. The state in general experiences cool weather conditions soon after the northeast monsoon in October / November, which lasts till middle of February. During this time the hilly regions of the Western Ghats like Munnar, Wayanad, Nelliyampathy, etc. experience considerable drop in temperature, sometimes dipping to freezing conditions. Munnar in recent years recorded -1 degree Celsius. In other places in Kerala, the temperature during this period ranges between 27 and 30 degree Celsius.

What are the Adventure Sports Opportunities in Kerala?

Hills, plantations, grasslands and countryside offer amazing trekking opportunities. Some of the most popular trekking destinations are Agasthyakoodam, Parambikulam and Munnar. Agasthyakoodam is the second highest peak in southern India. It is located at a distance of 70 km from Thiruvananthapuram. The trekking experience to Agasthyakoodam is one for a lifetime. There are two options at Parambikulam. There is the forest tramway trek and the Kariyanshola trail through evergreen forests. In Munnar, the trek to Meesapulimala is the most sought-after choice. It takes six hours to trek through forest to Meesapulimala, and it is quite a demanding but thrilling trek.

The Five Best Hill Stations

Munnar Undoubtedly Munnar is the most sought-after hill station in Kerala. It is known for its sprawling tea plantations, misty hills and cool climate. Enjoy scenic boat rides on Kundala lake, witness majestic waterfalls like Echo Point, or trek through Shola forests teeming with diverse flora and fauna. Munnar caters to all types of travellers, from honeymooners seeking romantic getaways to families in search of bonding- and fun time to those raring for adventure outings. Wayanad This verdant place on the Western Ghats is known for thick rainforests, wildlife sanctuaries and cascading waterfalls. You can spot elephants and tigers in the Wayanad Wildlife Sanctuary, go exploring in ancient caves, or embark on thrilling hikes through lush greenery. Wayanad offers a perfect escape for Nature lovers and adventure seekers. The Chembra Peak, Banasura Sagar Dam and Edakkal Caves are some of the must-visit attractions in this region.

What makes Kerala's traditional architecture unique?

Kerala's traditional architecture is unique due to its harmonious blend of aesthetics, functionality and environmental adaptation. Rooted in the principles of Vastu Shastra, Kerala’s unique architectural style reflects the region’s climate, culture and rich heritage, making it a unique representation of sustainable design. One of the most striking features of Kerala’s architecture is the sloping roof designed to withstand heavy monsoon rains. Constructed with locally sourced materials like clay tiles and timber, these roofs not only ensure effective water drainage but also enhance the structure’s thermal efficiency, keeping interiors cool during humid summers.
